Here is the script, The royal chamber. Hamlet is pacing back and forth, while King Claudius and Queen Gertrude observe :

Hamlet:(frustrated) "I can't believe it! Someone has taken my favorite hat!"

King Claudius: (amused) "Oh, Hamlet, it's just a hat. I'm sure you'll find it."

Queen Gertrude: (sympathetically) "Don't worry, dear. Maybe it's just misplaced. We'll help you look for it."

Hamlet: (grinning) "Look! I found it! Oh, wait... that's your crown, Uncle."

King Claudius: (laughing) "Oh, Hamlet, you never fail to surprise me. That's indeed the royal crown."

Queen Gertrude: (teasingly) "Hamlet, I think you might need some glasses. That crown is a bit too big for a hat."?

Hamlet: (sheepishly) "Well, it does look quite majestic on me, don't you think?"

King Claudius: (playfully) "Yes, my dear prince, but I believe we should keep the crown where it belongs, on my head."

Queen Gertrude: (smiling) "Come now, Hamlet, let's continue searching for your missing hat. It must be here somewhere."

Hamlet: (excitedly) "I found it! Here it is! My beloved hat!"

King Claudius: (relieved) "Ah, there you go, Hamlet. See? It was right under your nose."

Queen Gertrude: (teasingly) "Or on top of your head, to be more precise."

Hamlet: (grinning) "Thank you both for your help. I can't imagine ruling with a crown on my head and a hat on my mind!"
